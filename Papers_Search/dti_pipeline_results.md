PREPROCESSING STEPS (in order):
- **Scaling and rotation**: Scans were scaled to a common resolution of 2.50 mm/px in three dimensions and rotated according to the RPS direction (left to right, anterior to posterior, inferior to superior).  
- **Brain mask extraction**: A brain mask was created using the Otsu thresholding method followed by a dilation operation to separate the brain volume from the background.  
- **Diffusion tensor fitting**: The ordinary least squares method was applied with corresponding b-values and b-vectors to fit a diffusion tensor (3×3 symmetric matrix) to each voxel.  
- **Fractional anisotropy (FA) calculation**: FA was computed from the eigenvalues (λ₁, λ₂, λ₃) of the diffusion tensor using the formula:  
  $ FA = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)^2 + (\lambda_1 - \lambda_3)^2 + (\lambda_2 - \lambda_3)^2} / (\lambda_1^2 + \lambda_2^2 + \lambda_3^2) $.  
- **Image cropping**: Final images were cropped to a size of 96 × 96 × 64 pixels.  

TOOLS MENTIONED:
- **Dipy** (for diffusion MRI analysis).

- Common resolution: 2.50 mm/px.  
- Cropped image size: 96 × 96 × 64 pixels.  
- Otsu thresholding with dilation.  
- Minimum number of volumes (shells): 10.
